Precision Control with Dr. Lin

By A. Rios • December 2024

Programmable qubits are leaving the page of research papers and entering manufacturable systems. We sat down with Dr. Lin to unpack how control stacks and cryogenic hardware are maturing in parallel.

The conversation spans firmware, calibration, and the path to fault tolerance. Key engineering decisions now hinge on how quickly the control plane can adapt to new error models while keeping coherence budgets intact.

“Speed matters, but repeatability matters more. Every control instruction should carry a proof of stability.”

Signal Integrity at Scale

Scaling from 100 to 1,000 qubits changes how we think about signal hygiene. Crosstalk moves from a theoretical concern to a daily debugging ritual, and the firmware stack has to expose the right observability hooks.

Firmware That Learns

Lin’s team is experimenting with adaptive firmware layers. The control software can retune drive pulses in response to drift, while guardrails enforce safe bounds for amplitude and frequency sweeps.

Next steps involve tighter co-design between superconducting materials, control logic, and layout. The result is a tighter feedback loop between fabrication, calibration, and runtime corrections.
